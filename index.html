About Interactive Media Design

The Interactive Media Design course offers an in-depth exploration of the multifaceted world of digital experiences, where creativity and technology converge to shape how users engage with content across various platforms. Throughout the semester, students will immerse themselves in the fundamental principles and practices that underpin effective interactive design, delving into a wide array of media forms such as websites, mobile applications, video games, and immersive installations. This course is structured to facilitate a hands-on approach to learning, encouraging students to engage in a series of collaborative workshops and individual projects that emphasize the importance of user experience (UX) design and interface design.

Client Project

Brothers in arms Memorial Site

the Students of Interactive Media Design are tasked with creating a website for the Shrishti Foundation, in order to Raise awareness and donations for the construction and installation of a stone memorial depicting scenes from joint battles fought by Indian and Canadian soldiers. This site will help to Ensure the sacrifices of these soldiers are remembered and honored. with this site, and the memorial it will help build, the Shirshti foundation will hope to Inspire future generations with the values of humanity and freedom through the commemoration of this shared history.

What will be the purpose of this project?
During World War I, over a million Indian soldiers served in various theaters of the war, including Europe, Africa, and the Middle East, as part of the British Indian Army. Indian and Canadian soldiers often fought side by side in key battles on the Western Front, such as Ypres, Neuve Chapelle, the Somme, and Vimy Ridge. If Indian troops in the Lahore Division hadn’t arrived in October and November of 1914 in the Flanders area of Belgium, the Germans would have taken the ports and the Canadian Expeditionary Force would not have landed on the European mainland. John McCrae’s poem In Flanders Field would not have been written. The Flanders’ territory was basically held by the British and Indians in the first battle of Ypres. Six months later, Canadians landed and then fought jointly with Indian soldiers in the second battle of Ypres. The memorial will provide a site for quiet reflection and inspiration, reminding future generations of the selfless sacrifice and valour of our ancestors, Indians & Canadian soldiers, who were brothers-in-arms, fighting alongside each other. Eventually over a few years, we envision the site to be transformed into a well maintained beautiful green space with a beautiful stone memorial, trails, benches and display along the trail and the memorial that will tell the untold stories of these soldiers and a testimony of the friendship and ties of India & Canada. The stormwater pond will add to the ambiance of the site, turning it into a park for quiet reflection, paying our respects and celebrations (during Remembrance day etc.)
